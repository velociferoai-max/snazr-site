How to Stop Being Called Quiet at Work

Small, repeatable habits that change what people notice—without pretending to be someone else.

1) Speak Early (Not All the Time)

Say one short comment or question in the first 5 minutes of a meeting. That tiny moment resets how people see you for the rest of the meeting.

2) Use Tiny, Low-Energy Small Talk

As you pass coworkers: “How did that project go?” or “Did you see the new update?” These micro-interactions quietly build presence without draining you.

3) Reframe “Quiet” as “Focused”

If someone says, “You’re quiet,” smile: “I like to think before I speak—it helps me make better points.” You just rebranded yourself.

4) Exit Chats Smoothly

Try: “Great chat—gotta jump back to this task, but let’s catch up later.” Confident, kind, and clean.
